The market wrote smaller this week. I do not count the headlines — I count the markets where
all four lights burn the same color: the **season**, the **producers' positioning**, the
**hedging program**, and the **term structure**. Where those four fall together, the line of
least resistance lies open and a man only has to follow it. Where three stand and one still
waits, I keep my hand in my pocket. This week the board shows three full hands — one long,
two short.

## WTI Crude Oil — long, 4/4

**Last 90.54 · week −2.69%**

| Season | Producer net | Hedging program | Structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| bullish (through Jun 14) | +358,016 (long) | upper half of range | **premium** — CLN26 90.54 vs CLQ26 87.99, +2.55 |

![WTI Crude Oil — bullish 4/4 setup]({{ page.cards }}/wti_crude.png)

A down week *inside* a bullish full house — and that is exactly the kind of week that tries the
man, not the market. Look under the price. The structure stands in backwardation: the front
month trades at a premium to the next, and a market does not pay up for the prompt barrel when
it is drowning in oil. The producers' book sits net long and high in its six-month range — it
has eased off the late-May peak (372k → 358k), but a holder, not a seller. And the bounce from
the upper 80s to 90 was carried on full volume, near 1.26 million for the week — the rise was
*confirmed*, not a thin drift. So I read this week's slip for what it is: a **reaction** on
ordinary volume that has not broken the prior pivot, the sort of pullback a man sits through
rather than sells into. But the season's support runs out **June 14** — the tailwind here is
borrowed time, and I treat it that way.

## The discipline of the week

Four lights are a permission, not a promise. Crude fell inside its bullish frame; wheat fell
with conviction; orange juice fell in the thin. The direction was not the difference — the
**volume and the structure underneath it** were. That is the whole of it: wait until all four
agree, then ask the tape to confirm the move before you trust it, and size to the liquidity that
is actually there. The big money has never been in the buying or the selling. It has been in
the waiting, and in the sitting once you are right.
